## Rationale

**1 — Field-to-role mapper (Quick win).** Directly targets the most common pain point (5 of 8 interviewees), but it's a content/recommendation tool — curate a role database and example paths, no matching infrastructure or ongoing operations needed.

**2 — Try-it micro-sprints (Big bet).** Highest-impact solution in the set — it's the literal mechanism P7 described for how clarity actually formed. Effort is pulled up by needing curated trial-task content across many fields plus a way to track completion.

**3 — Niche-tag directory (Big bet, least efficient).** Addresses a real, named bottleneck (4 of 8), but a directory is only as useful as its supply side — recruiting and onboarding enough tagged mentors/peers to reach critical mass is a cold-start problem, which is why effort is highest here even though impact is moderate.

**4 — Warm-tie / near-peer matching (top Quick win).** Every example of mentoring that actually worked in the research was a warm tie — this is the single best-evidenced mechanism in the whole synthesis. It's a matching policy change more than new infrastructure, and near-peers are easier to recruit than senior cross-sector professionals — hence high impact, low effort.

**5 — Structured mentoring track (Big bet, most expensive).** The only model in the data with a proven track record (11 of 13 placed), but replicating it means running a service: screening, multi-month case management, mentor retention, logistics support. High impact, but the heaviest lift of the six.

**6 — Async AI coordinator (Fill-in).** Solves a real but narrower pain point (the coach's scheduling/Burmese-language gap, P1 and P4's fatigue with repeated intro conversations). It's a software build using existing AI/calendar APIs — moderate effort for a supporting tool, not a core-problem solver.

## Read

Two clear quick wins (warm-tie matching, field-to-role mapper) — start here. Three big bets are all worth pursuing but sequence by what the org can sustain operationally; the structured mentoring track has the best proof point but the highest cost. The AI coordinator is a fill-in: low risk, worth doing once a quick win is shipped, not before.
