# Opportunity Solution Tree — Myanmar Career Mentorship

**Desired outcome:** Provide a clear career path for people who are navigating their career with uncertainty.

Built from the 9-interview synthesis (8 students/grads + 1 working mentor/coach). Each opportunity and solution below is tied to specific evidence rather than assumption.

---

## Opportunity 1 — They can name a broad field, but not a specific job title, the skills it needs, or a timeline to get there

**Evidence:** P1 ("I don't have a clear goal"), P2 (unsure if it's "too late" to switch tracks), P3 ("difficult to identify which title/job I want to work on"), P4 (torn between researcher / policy practitioner / land-issues educator), P8 (torn between 3-5 paths: NLP, computer vision, further study).

### Solution 1.1 — Field-to-role explorer
An interactive tool that takes someone's broad interest + background and surfaces concrete job titles, mapped against real example paths of people who started from the same interest area.

### Solution 1.2 — "Try-it" micro-sprints
Structured 1-2 week trial tasks for a candidate role, paired with a skills-and-timeline checklist — so clarity comes from doing the work briefly, not just discussing it (directly suggested by P7: "try the suggested career path for one or two weeks in practice").

---

## Opportunity 2 — They can't find or reach someone who has actually walked their specific, narrow path

**Evidence:** P3 got her job through a personal contact, not an application; P4 is "tired of introducing myself" and self-solved by re-reading her own CVs for patterns; P6 had to cross-reference Facebook posts against LinkedIn to identify one usable contact; P8 can't find anyone on her exact sub-path even inside large IT-adjacent Facebook groups because people don't label their specialization.

### Solution 2.1 — Niche-tagged mentor/peer directory
Searchable profiles tagged by narrow sub-field, exact role, and career stage — not broad sector — closing the gap left by LinkedIn/Facebook where specialization is invisible.

### Solution 2.2 — Warm-tie / near-peer matching
Prioritize matching with people 1-3 years ahead on the same narrow path over senior, cross-sector "experts." Every example of mentoring that actually worked in the research was a warm tie (P5's cousin, P8's teacher, P7's self-found specific contact) — not a stranger-professional match.

---

## Opportunity 3 — A single advice conversation doesn't produce clarity; sustained, structured engagement does

**Evidence:** P7 (most mentoring exposure of anyone interviewed): "meeting a mentor alone does not automatically solve career problems." P1: a mentor "doesn't know my background, they can't give concrete advice." P8's one formal program had "no immediate impact" due to mismatched background and timing. The coach's program — the one model in the data that's actually working — runs 4-6 months with screening, milestones, and logistics support (CV, interviews, onboarding), not light-touch conversations.

### Solution 3.1 — Structured multi-month mentoring track
Milestones, a skills checklist, and scheduled check-ins, modeled on the coach's program (40 applicants screened to 13/year, 11 of the most recent 13 got jobs).

### Solution 3.2 — Async AI coordinator
Auto-generates the skills/timeline roadmap for a target role, nudges follow-up tasks, and works in Burmese — for people who want structure without repeated, draining intro conversations (P1's reluctance to talk to mentors, P4's "tired of introducing myself," and the coach's named pain point that Notion AI doesn't support Burmese).
